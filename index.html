01 — The Approach
Hiring Manager Partnerships

The Whole Process. Not Just the Search.

  • The recruiting process is owned end to end so hiring managers can stay focused on their work
  • Volume hiring and niche searches are not solved the same way.
  • Market intel and interview guidance are built into the process to sharpen decisions at every stage
Multi-Channel Sourcing

Job Boards Are Not a Strategy

  • Market research identifies where target talent lives, what they're being paid, and what it takes to move them
  • Sourcing happens through events, conferences, and direct networking
  • Sometimes the right hire is already on the team. Career coaching skills surface where talent is being underused before the search begins.
Relationship-Focused Recruiting

Recruiting With Retention in Mind

  • Candidate pipelines are built around future needs, not just the open req
  • After the hire, the work continues with check-ins for both candidates and hiring managers to make sure the placement sticks
  • Placements are treated as relationships, not transactions.
Operations Management

Systems That Outlast the Engagement

  • Recruiting data dashboards that track what's working and show where to go next.
  • Playbooks, Meet n' Greet programs, and steering committees built to outlast the engagement.
  • ATS architecture, job descriptions, and skill mapping designed for long-term use.
